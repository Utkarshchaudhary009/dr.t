# 🎨 dr.t — Slide-by-Slide Strategy

**Total slides: 8 | Total time: 5 minutes**
**Design rule: Dark background. Maximum 2 font sizes per slide. One big idea per slide. No walls of text.**

---

## 🎨 Global Design System

Before building slides, set these once:

| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| **Background** | Deep navy / near-black `#0D1117` or `#0A0E1A` |
| **Primary accent** | Teal/cyan `#00D4AA` — use for key numbers and highlighted words |
| **Secondary accent** | Soft white `#F0F6FF` for body text |
| **Danger/warning color** | Warm amber `#FF8C42` — used on problem slides |
| **Font — Headings** | [Outfit](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Outfit) Bold |
| **Font — Body** | [Inter](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter) Regular |
| **Logo** | `dr.t` in teal, lowercase, monospace feel — top-left corner on every slide |

## Slide 1 — Title / Hook

**⏱ Time on slide: ~40 seconds (you're speaking, not reading)**

---

### Headline (center, massive text)
```
"In my house, chai is not
a morning ritual.

It's a monthly celebration."
```
> Font size: 52–60pt. Bold. White text. One line per phrase for rhythm.

### Sub-headline (below, smaller)
```
— Three generations of diabetes. One idea.
```
> 24pt. Teal color. Italic.

### Bottom strip
```
dr.t  •  AI Food Advisor for Diabetes  •  [Team Name]  •  [School Name]
```
> 14pt. Muted white. Thin divider line above it.

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
**Full-bleed background image:**
A dimly lit Indian kitchen or dining table — a steel cup of chai placed on a wooden table, shot from above (flat lay). Warm, emotional, slightly desaturated to not overpower text. You want viewers to *feel* something before you say a word.

> **Where to get it:** Search Unsplash for "chai cup wooden table india" or "morning tea india flat lay". Pick one that feels intimate and home-like, not stock-photo perfect.

> **Overlay:** Add a dark gradient overlay on the image (left side darker, right lighter) so white text is legible.

### Speaker Notes for This Slide
> Start speaking BEFORE the slide advances. Walk up. Let the image appear. Let the room see it for 2 seconds. Then begin: *"In my house, three people have diabetes — my father, my grandfather, and my grandmother. Chai is not a morning ritual in our house. It's a monthly celebration. That's what diabetes does — it turns everyday things into events. Every meal starts with a question: 'Can I eat this?' We built dr.t to answer that question."*

## Slide 2 — The Problem (Scale)

**⏱ Time on slide: ~30 seconds**

---

### Headline
```
The Scale of the Problem
```
> 40pt. White.

### Big Stats (center, massive — the stats ARE the visual)

```
      77 Million
   Indians with Diabetes

      460 Million
   Worldwide — and growing
```
> Numbers: 90–100pt, Teal (`#00D4AA`)
> Labels: 22pt, White

### Body text (below stats, 3 bullet points, concise)
```
→  #1 daily challenge: deciding what to eat
→  No nutritionist. No instant answer.
→  Current solutions? They require too much effort.
```
> 18pt. Soft white. Left-aligned.

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
**Right side of the slide (split layout — 50/50):**

Option A: A simple map of India with a glowing dot density pattern showing diabetes prevalence by region. (Can be made free in Canva or Datawrapper.)

Option B: A bold **infographic-style number block** — just the two numbers, very large, stacked, with a thin teal horizontal rule between them. No map. Clean.

> **Recommendation: Option B.** Cleaner, faster for judges to absorb.

### Design Notes
- No paragraph text. Numbers do all the talking.
- The word "growing" should be in amber/orange `#FF8C42` to signal danger.

## Slide 3 — The Real Problem (App Fatigue + Accessibility)

**⏱ Time on slide: ~40 seconds**

---

### Headline
```
The Existing Solutions Are Failing Them
```
> 36pt. White.

### Left column — "The App Graveyard"
```
📱 Downloaded.
    Used for a week.
    Forgotten.

We call it App Fatigue.
```
> 22pt. White. Casual, punchy rhythm.

### Right column — Two contrasting personas

```
👤 You (20s)          👴 Your Grandfather (70s)

✓ Download an app    ✗ "Which app store?"
✓ Create account     ✗ "I forgot my password"
✓ Verify OTP         ✗ "The SMS didn't come"
✓ Learn new UI       ✗ Gives up.
```
> Use a simple 2-column comparison. Teal checkmarks. Amber X marks.

### Bottom — The Insight
```
The people who need this most
can't use the tools we've built for them.
```
> 26pt. Bold. Teal. Centered at the bottom.

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
**Background or side panel:**
A split image — Left: a young person on their phone (modern, app-filled). Right: an elderly person looking confused at a phone screen.

> **Canva search:** "elderly person confused phone" + "young person smartphone"
> Desaturate images slightly. Dark overlay to keep text readable.
> OR: Skip photos. Use the comparison table as the only visual — it's strong enough.

### Speaker Notes
> *"The apps exist. MyFitnessPal, HealthifyMe, sugar trackers. But here's the reality — how many of you have downloaded a health app you no longer use? That's app fatigue. Now imagine your grandfather, trying to navigate an OTP screen just to log what he ate for breakfast. He gives up. And he's exactly the person who needs this most."*

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
**Right side (or center-right):**
A clean Telegram chat mockup showing:

## Slide 5 — Live Demo

**⏱ Time on slide: ~90–120 seconds (the longest slide)**

---

### Headline
```
Let's see it live.
```
> 52pt. Bold. White. Dead center on the slide.

### Below headline — just one line
```
[ Switching to Telegram now → ]
```
> 22pt. Teal. Subtle arrow animation if your tool supports it.

### Bottom strip — Demo checklist visible to YOU (small text, bottom of slide)
```
1. /start → greeting    2. Healthy food photo    3. Unhealthy food photo    4. "How much sugar today?"
```
> 13pt. Muted gray. This is your personal reminder visible from the podium. Judges are looking at the phone screen.

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
**Full slide background:**
A large, slightly blurred screenshot of the Telegram interface (your actual bot conversation). Overlaid with a subtle "LIVE" badge in the top-right corner (red dot + "LIVE" text).

**OR** — the slide is almost entirely black with just the headline and a large blinking cursor `_` animation to signal "this is happening live."

### Speaker Notes
> *"Instead of telling you what it does — let me just show you."* [Put phone on projector. Begin demo. Do NOT read from slides during this period.]

> 🔴 **This slide stays up the entire demo. Don't advance it.**

## Slide 6 — How It Works (Technical)

**⏱ Time on slide: ~60 seconds**

---

### Headline
```
Not a Chatbot. An AI Agent.
```
> 40pt. Bold. White. The word "Agent" in teal.

### Architecture Diagram (center of slide)

```
┌────────────┐    ┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────────────┐    ┌──────────┐
│  Telegram  │───▶│  Next.js     │───▶│  MedGemma 4b     │───▶│ Supabase │
│   User     │◀───│  Webhook API │◀───│  AI Agent        │◀───│ Database │
└────────────┘    └──────────────┘    └──────────────────┘    └──────────┘
                                              │
                                    ┌─────────┴──────────┐
                                    │   Agent Tools       │
                                    │  • recordSugar()    │
                                    │  • retrieveSugar()  │
                                    └────────────────────┘
```
> Render this as a proper designed flow diagram in Canva/Figma/Excalidraw. Use boxes with rounded corners, teal connecting arrows, dark background. Keep it clean — 5 boxes max.

### Three callout boxes (below diagram, side by side)

```
┌──────────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────────┐
│  🧬 MedGemma 4b      │  │  🔧 Tool-Calling      │  │  🔄 Dual Fallback    │
│                      │  │                      │  │                      │
│  Medical domain      │  │  AI autonomously      │  │  Gemini primary.     │
│  model. Not ChatGPT. │  │  logs to database.   │  │  Ollama backup.      │
│  Built for health.   │  │  Not just text.      │  │  Always available.   │
└──────────────────────┘  └──────────────────────┘  └──────────────────────┘
```
> Teal border on each box. Icon at top. 16pt text inside. These three boxes are your "technical wow" moment.

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
The **architecture diagram** IS the visual. Keep the rest of the slide clean.

> Optional: Add a tiny MedGemma logo or Google DeepMind attribution next to the AI box to establish credibility.

### Speaker Notes
> *"Most hackathon projects call ChatGPT and display the response. That's a chatbot. dr.t is an agent — when you send a food photo, it doesn't just reply. It makes a decision to write your sugar intake to the database. When you ask 'how much today?' it queries the database autonomously. And we use MedGemma 4b — a model trained specifically on medical data, not a general-purpose one."*

## Slide 8 — Close / Thank You

**⏱ Time on slide: ~20 seconds (then Q&A)**

---

### Center — Large, minimal

```
        dr.t

"Healthcare shouldn't
 require an appointment."

        Questions?
```
> `dr.t` — 80pt, Teal, Bold.
> Tagline — 28pt, White, Italic.
> "Questions?" — 24pt, Amber.

### Bottom strip (small)
```
🤖 @dr_t_bot on Telegram  •  github.com/[your-repo]  •  Deployed on Vercel
```
> 14pt. Muted white. This gives judges something to scan/note/visit after.

### 🖼️ Image / Visual
**Full bleed background — same as Slide 1 (the chai cup).**

You open and close with the same image. This creates a **narrative circle** — the problem you opened with now has a solution. Judges feel the closure even if they can't articulate why.

> This is a classic storytelling technique. It works.

### Speaker Notes
> Don't say "thank you." Don't say "that's it." Just: *"We'd love your questions."* Stand confident. Let the silence sit for 2 seconds first.

## ⚡ The 3 Non-Negotiable Design Rules

1. **One idea per slide.** If you're tempted to add more, make a new slide.
2. **If a judge can read the slide in 2 seconds — it's too text-heavy.** Slides support your speech, they don't replace it.
3. **The demo slide (Slide 5) must be visually different** from all others — it signals a mode change from "presentation" to "proof."
